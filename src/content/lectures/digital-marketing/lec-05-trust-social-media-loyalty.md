# The Role of Trust in Understanding the Impact of Social Media Marketing on Brand Equity and Brand Loyalty

> Part of: [[Digital Marketing]]
> **Topic 5 — Content Marketing** · Reading
> Citation: Ebrahim, R. S. (2020). The role of trust in understanding the impact of social media marketing on brand equity and brand loyalty. *Journal of Relationship Marketing*, 19(4), 287–308. https://doi.org/10.1080/15332667.2019.1705742
> Key concepts: [[Social Media Marketing]], [[Brand Trust]], [[Brand Equity]], [[Brand Loyalty]], [[SMM Activities]]

---

## TL;DR
An empirical study (online survey of 287 telecom-following social media users in Egypt, analysed with **structural equation modelling**) testing how **social media marketing (SMM) activities** drive brand loyalty through brand trust and brand equity. Although the literature usually identifies five SMM dimensions, here only **three load** — **trendiness**, **customisation** and **word-of-mouth**. SMM has a direct positive effect on brand trust and brand loyalty, but **not** directly on brand equity; its effect on equity is *fully mediated* by **brand trust**, and its effect on loyalty is *partially mediated* by trust. The headline message: trust is the focal mechanism translating SMM effort into brand value and loyalty.

## Why It's on the Reading List
It is the quantitative, content/social-media reading for the course: it operationalises "what good social content does" (the SMM activity dimensions) and shows the **causal chain SMM → trust → equity → loyalty**. Ideal for exam questions on measuring SMM effectiveness and the role of trust.

## Background & Research Question
Social media is now integrated into marketing strategy (marketers expect 71% of budget growth in social spend), but measuring the **impact of these practices on brand value and customer relationships remains under-studied** — especially from the perspective of *branding* literature. Prior work clusters into brand communities, e-WOM, network analysis and product-harm crises, and tends to study luxury fashion or tourism.

The study addresses this gap: investigate the effect of SMM activities on positive consumer behavioural responses toward the brand — specifically **brand equity** and **brand loyalty** — and the mediating role of **brand trust**.

## Key Concepts & Definitions

> [!info] Definition — Social media marketing (SMM)
> The process that utilises social media technologies and channels to create, communicate and deliver marketing offerings that enhance stakeholders' value (Tuten & Solomon; Yadav & Rahman). It is an *augmentation* to traditional/tradigital tools — built on **pull/attraction** messaging, not the interruption-disruption model.

> [!info] Definition — SMM activities (the five dimensions)
> Effective communication methods capturing engaged consumers' perceptions of marketers' practices on social media (Kim & Ko, 2010/2012):
>
> | Dimension | Meaning |
> |-----------|---------|
> | **Entertainment** | The hedonic aspect — fun and pleasure from social media use |
> | **Interaction** | Users' contribution to brands; two-way conversation |
> | **Trendiness** | Information is new and up-to-date |
> | **Customisation** | Extent the service satisfies different customer tastes/demands |
> | **Word-of-mouth** | Informal opinion exchange and recommendation among users |

> [!info] Definition — Brand equity
> The incremental / added value of the brand (Srivastava & Shocker). Customer-based brand equity = "the differential effect of brand knowledge on consumer response to the marketing of the brand" (Keller, 1993). The study uses *overall* brand equity (Yoo & Donthu).

> [!info] Definition — Brand trust
> The willingness of a consumer to rely on the ability of a brand to perform as entitled, involving the firm's benevolence to act in the customer's best interest (safety, reliability) — Chaudhuri & Holbrook. Especially important online where "workable rules" are absent.

> [!info] Definition — Brand loyalty
> A deeply held commitment to rebuy the brand regardless of situational factors (Chaudhuri & Holbrook). Has a **behavioural** facet (repurchase → market share) and an **attitudinal** facet (preference/commitment → higher relative brand value).

## Main Arguments / Findings

**Hypotheses tested (6 paths):**

| Hypothesis | Path | Result |
|------------|------|--------|
| H1 | SMM → Brand Trust | **Supported** (β ≈ 0.24, p = .000) |
| H2 | SMM → Brand Equity | **Rejected** (β ≈ 0.07, p = .218) |
| H3 | SMM → Brand Loyalty | **Supported** (β ≈ 0.17, p = .006) |
| H4 | Brand Trust → Brand Equity | **Supported** (β ≈ 0.63, p = .000) |
| H5 | Brand Trust → Brand Loyalty | **Supported** (β ≈ 0.23, p = .005) |
| H6 | Brand Equity → Brand Loyalty | **Supported** (β ≈ 0.30, p = .000) |

> [!example] The mediation result (the heart of the paper)
> - SMM → Brand Equity is **insignificant directly**, but the indirect path **SMM → Trust → Equity is significant** (≈ 0.15, p = .009) → **full mediation** by brand trust.
> - SMM → Loyalty is significant directly but drops (from 0.17 to ~0.12) once trust is controlled → **partial mediation** by brand trust.

> [!warning] Only three of five SMM dimensions survived
> Exploratory + confirmatory factor analysis dropped **entertainment** and **interaction** (poor standardised loadings). Only **trendiness, customisation and word-of-mouth** remained — i.e. users engage to get tailored services, the latest information, and to share experiences/recommendations.

**Why trust is central:** because of privacy concerns and information sharing, trust governs use of and participation in social media. Trust is the *catalyst* establishing long-term relationships and the *proxy to equity* that carves a unique, differentiated brand value into consumers' minds. Firm- or user-generated communications increase trust; brand communities can be perceived as more trustworthy than traditional PR (Schmidt & Iyer).

## Methodology
- **Design**: self-administered online questionnaire (SurveyHero), items adapted from prior literature; face/content validity via expert + pilot panel.
- **Sample**: 287 valid responses (157 male, 130 female) from Egyptian users following one of four telecommunications companies; 69.5% under 40; mostly Facebook/Twitter (70.7%). Non-probability sampling.
- **Measures**: 7-point Likert. SMM = Kim & Ko's 11-item scale; brand trust 5 items (Chahal & Rani); brand equity 4 items (Delgado-Ballester & Munuera-Alemán); loyalty 4 items (Chaudhuri & Holbrook). Cronbach's α 0.78–0.93.
- **Analysis**: EFA (PCA, VARIMAX) → CFA (maximum likelihood) → **SEM** with 6 structural paths. Model fit acceptable (e.g. CFI ≈ 0.94, GFI ≈ 0.92, RMSEA ≈ 0.08); convergent and discriminant validity supported (AVE > squared inter-construct correlations).

## Framework / Model
The proposed conceptual model places **brand trust as the mediator** between SMM activities and the two outcome constructs:

```
SMM activities ──► Brand Trust ──► Brand Equity
       │                 │              │
       │                 └────────────► Brand Loyalty ◄──┘
       └──────────────────────────────►
```

In words: SMM activities feed brand trust; trust drives both brand equity and brand loyalty; brand equity itself feeds brand loyalty. The *direct* SMM → equity link is absent — equity is reached **only through trust**.

## Implications for Marketers

- **Lead with the three working levers**: keep content **fresh and up-to-date (trendiness)**, **personalise** service to customer tastes (customisation), and **fuel WOM** (let customers share experiences, exchange opinions and recommend).
- **Trust is the engine**: SMM effort only converts into brand equity *through* trust — so cultivate credible, controlled digital content and authentic brand communities.
- Invite customers to share *why* they chose the brand to raise the trustworthiness of generated content.
- SMM sits within **relationship/customer-relationship marketing** — its payoff is loyalty, more sales and profit, but its effectiveness should be measured via users' behavioural responses.

## Exam Takeaways
> [!tip] Likely exam points
> - **Causal chain**: SMM → Brand Trust → Brand Equity / Brand Loyalty (trust = mediator).
> - **Full mediation** of SMM→equity by trust; **partial mediation** of SMM→loyalty by trust; **no direct** SMM→equity effect.
> - The five SMM dimensions (entertainment, interaction, trendiness, customisation, WOM) — but here **only trendiness, customisation & WOM** held.
> - Definitions: brand trust, brand equity (Keller's customer-based equity), behavioural vs. attitudinal loyalty.
> - Method label: **online survey, n = 287, Egypt telecom, SEM**.
> - Social media = **pull/attraction** messaging vs. traditional **interruption-disruption**.

## Summary
- SMM activities measured on Kim & Ko's scale; only trendiness, customisation and WOM loaded reliably.
- SMM has direct positive effects on brand trust and brand loyalty, but not directly on brand equity.
- Brand trust fully mediates SMM→equity and partially mediates SMM→loyalty.
- Brand equity in turn drives brand loyalty.
- Trust is the key mechanism converting social media effort into brand value and loyalty.
