> [!info] How to use this plan
> No exam date is baked in, so this is structured as **four passes**, not four weeks — map them onto your calendar once the date is known and shrink Pass 1 before you shrink Pass 3. The ordering inside each pass follows the [[High-Yield Topics — What the Exams Actually Test|frequency analysis]]: two-period consumption, production/TFP, investment/user cost and labor supply carry both 32-point long questions on every Moed paper, so they get first claim on your time. This is a hard exam and the syllabus is wide — the plan works by being ruthless about what earns marks, not by covering everything equally.

> [!tip] Use the site properly while you revise
> - **Status pills**: every problem-set and past-paper question has a status pill — mark each attempt (nailed it / shaky / wrong) as you go, so Pass 4 can target exactly the questions that hurt.
> - **Dictionary hover previews**: hover any linked term (like [[euler-equation]] or [[public-debt-dynamics]]) for the definition without leaving the page — faster than re-opening lecture notes mid-question.
> - **Show-solution toggles**: past-paper questions hide their solutions behind a per-question toggle. In Pass 3, answer on paper *before* toggling — the toggle is the honesty test.
> - **🗣️ In plain English recaps**: every macro solution ends with a plain-English recap. If the algebra clicked but the *point* didn't, read that box before moving on.

## Pass 1 — Foundations (1–2 days)

Goal: know the toolkit and the map before touching any questions. This pass is deliberately short — reading is not revising.

The final is **open-notes with the formula sheet attached**, so nothing needs memorising — but you must know *which* block solves *which* question shape without hunting. That recognition is what Pass 1 builds.

- [ ] Read the [[Macro Equation Sheet]] top to bottom. For each block, say out loud what kind of exam question it solves (the [[High-Yield Topics — What the Exams Actually Test|high-yield page]] tells you exactly which past-paper questions each block has powered).
- [ ] Dictionary sweep of the high-yield terms — hover or open each and check you could explain it to a classmate: [[euler-equation]], [[consumption-smoothing]], [[marginal-product-of-capital]], [[capital-accumulation-equation]], [[labor-supply]], [[income-effect]], [[solow-residual]], [[development-accounting]], [[public-debt-dynamics]], [[primary-deficit]], [[job-finding-rate]], [[separation-rate]], [[gdp-deflator]], [[cpi]], [[purchasing-power-parity]], [[gnp]], [[polarization]].
- [ ] Skim the lectures where you feel weakest — likely candidates given what the exams test: [[Lec_02-Consumption and Saving]], [[Lec_05-Investment]], [[Lec_07-Labor Market]], [[Lec_10-Fiscal Policy]]. Skim [[Lec_01-Data Review]], [[Lec_04-Production]], [[Lec_06-Equilibrium in the Goods Market]], [[Lec_08-Labor Market Data, Participation & Unemployment]] and [[Lec_09-Inequality & Polarization]] only where a dictionary term didn't click.
- [ ] Write the four "workhorse" results on one page, by hand, from the sheet: the Euler equation, the static labor-supply FOC, the user-cost condition, and debt dynamics. This page is your Pass 4 benchmark.

**Time estimate:** 4–8 hours. Do not let it become a week.

## Pass 2 — Problem sets by topic cluster (4–6 days)

Goal: fluency on every question *shape* the exams recycle. Work in **topic clusters**, not problem-set order, so each model gets one concentrated block.

> [!tip] The TA and extra questions are your fresh practice — use them last in each cluster
> You've only ever done the **submission questions** of each set, and the TA/extra questions are newly added to the site. That makes them the only substantial pool of questions you've never seen — the closest thing you have to fresh exam practice outside the past papers. In each cluster: redo the submission questions first (fast, to confirm the method), then do the TA/extra questions **blind, on paper, before opening the solution**. Mark every attempt with its status pill.

Order the clusters by exam weight:

- [ ] **Consumption & saving** — [[ps-2\|PS2]] (both questions, then the new extra material). Target: run the Lagrangian → Euler → substitute → solve pipeline without notes, for log *and* CRRA utility. This is the engine of a 32-point long question on **every** Moed paper.
- [ ] **Investment & goods market** — [[ps-4\|PS4]] (Q1–Q3 + TA questions). Target: given any five of {$p_k$, $p_k^f$, $r$, $\delta$, $\tau_K$, $A^f$}, solve the user-cost condition for the sixth; then shift $S$/$I$ curves and sign $r$ confidently.
- [ ] **Labor market & taxes** — [[ps-5\|PS5]] Q1–Q5 + the new Q6–Q8, then [[ps-6\|PS6]] Q1–Q5 (Q4–Q5 were TA-session — treat them as fresh). Target: derive $N(w)$ from the static FOC for any utility they throw at you, and narrate income vs substitution effects. Both Moed B long questions live here.
- [ ] **Fiscal & debt** — [[ps-6\|PS6]] Q3 (plus the tax questions already covered in the labor cluster). Target: the debt forecast recipe (Fisher for $r$, compound growth for $g$, then plug in) at speed.
- [ ] **Measurement** — [[ps-1\|PS1]] (Q1–Q4 redo, Q5–Q8 are TA questions you haven't done). Target: CPI vs deflator with a non-consumption good, PPP conversion, GDP/GNP/NFP signs. Cheap, reliable short-question points.
- [ ] **Production, TFP & allocation** — [[ps-3\|PS3]] (Q1–Q3 redo, Q4 growth accounting is extra), then [[ps-7\|PS7]] (efficient allocation of capital across firms — brand new, never submitted, so it's all fresh material and it trains the allocation MCQ on the [[Sample Exam 2026]]). Target: back out $\alpha$ from a factor share, then TFP as the residual — and *always differentiate the function actually given* (the additive-form trap).

**Time estimate:** roughly a day per cluster; consumption and labor deserve the fullest days. 25–35 hours total.

## Pass 3 — Past papers, hardest use (3–4 days)

Goal: exam performance, not understanding — understanding was Passes 1–2. The papers are scarce (five exist, and you've read solutions to some), so burn them carefully.

- [ ] **[[Moed A 2025]] — timed and blind.** 3 hours, on paper, formula sheet only, no site. Then mark yourself brutally against the worked solutions, one show-solution toggle at a time. Log every question's status pill.
- [ ] **[[Moed B 2025]] — timed and blind**, a day or two later. Same protocol. These two are the most recent and most predictive papers — treat them as the real thing.
- [ ] **[[Moed A 2023-24]] and [[Moed B 2023-24]]** — work them question-by-question (untimed is fine; you've likely seen parts). Focus on the twists you haven't met: the additive production function, the consumption-tax Lagrangian, the Laffer-curve long question.
- [ ] For **anything that didn't click**, read the 🗣️ In plain English recap under the solution, then the linked [[Macro Equation Sheet]] block, then the dictionary terms. If it *still* doesn't click, flag the topic for Pass 4 — don't stall the pass.
- [ ] **[[Sample Exam 2026]] last, as the dress rehearsal.** It's the only paper in your actual MCQ format — 20 questions, timed. Do it once everything else is done, in one sitting. MCQ distractors punish sign errors and dropped denominators, so slow down on the arithmetic you'd normally hand-wave.

**Time estimate:** ~4 hours per Moed paper (sit + mark), ~3 hours for the sample. 18–22 hours total.

> [!warning] Don't read solutions you haven't attempted
> A past paper you've read is a past paper you've spent. The show-solution toggle exists so you can grade one question at a time without spoiling the rest — use it that way.

## Pass 4 — Targeted repair (1–2 days, repeatable)

Goal: convert every red/amber status pill into green. This pass is a loop, not a stage — run it as many times as the calendar allows.

- [ ] Sort your misses by the [[High-Yield Topics — What the Exams Actually Test|frequency table]]: a wobble on two-period consumption outranks a wobble on PPP, whatever order you happened to miss them in.
- [ ] For each weak topic: re-derive the key result **from memory on a blank page** — the Euler equation from the Lagrangian, the labor-supply FOC to $N(w)$, the user-cost condition from the firm's problem, and one full debt-dynamics forecast. Check against your Pass 1 page.
- [ ] Redo the specific past-paper and problem-set questions you missed (the status pills are your queue). A question isn't repaired until you've re-done it cold, not just re-read it.
- [ ] Rebuild speed on the surviving weak spots with any TA/extra questions you left unused in Pass 2.
- [ ] Final evening: one last read of the [[Macro Equation Sheet]] and the plain-English recaps of the questions that hurt most. Then stop — sleep beats a fifth pass.

**Time estimate:** 6–10 hours per loop.

---

> [!success] The honest summary
> The exam is hard, but it is **predictable**: four topics power ~64 of 100 points every sitting, the same recipes recur with rotated unknowns, and you have a genuinely unseen question bank (the TA/extra questions) plus two blind-sittable 2025 papers. Foundations fast → clusters deep → papers blind → repair ruthlessly. That's the whole plan.
