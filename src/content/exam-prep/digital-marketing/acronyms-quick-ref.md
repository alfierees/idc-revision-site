# Acronyms & Key Terms

> The exam **explicitly tests acronyms** ("yes, you need to know the meaning of the acronyms…"). This is the master list — reading-derived terms confirmed from our notes, plus the standard digital-marketing metrics and models that recur in the past exams. Readings cram: [[_Exam Cram Sheet - Readings]].

---

## Media buying & pricing models

| Acronym | Stands for                    | Meaning                                                                                              |
| ------- | ----------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **CPM** | Cost Per Mille                | Cost per **1,000 impressions**; you pay for exposure/reach (low price, low engagement guarantee).    |
| **CPC** | Cost Per Click                | You pay only when a user **clicks** the ad.                                                          |
| **CPA** | Cost Per Acquisition / Action | You pay only when a defined **action/conversion** occurs (low pricing, high engagement requirement). |
| **CPL** | Cost Per Lead                 | You pay per qualified **lead** captured.                                                             |
| **CPV** | Cost Per View                 | You pay per **video view**.                                                                          |
| **PPC** | Pay Per Click                 | Pricing/bidding model where advertiser pays per click (e.g. Google Ads).                             |

> [!example] Past-exam application
> Influencers paid by **revenue generated via their coupon code** → this is a **CPA** model (pay on action), not CPC/CPL. (2024-A Q17)

## Performance metrics & KPIs

| Acronym | Stands for | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **KPI** | Key Performance Indicator | A metric chosen to track progress toward an objective. |
| **OMTM** | One Metric That Matters | The single most important metric to focus on at a given stage. |
| **CTR** | Click-Through Rate | Clicks ÷ impressions; ad engagement measure. |
| **CR** | Conversion Rate | Conversions ÷ visitors (or clicks). |
| **ROI** | Return On Investment | Net return relative to cost. |
| **ROAS** | Return On Ad Spend | Revenue generated per unit of ad spend. |
| **MAU / DAU** | Monthly / Daily Active Users | Count of unique active users in the period; engagement/retention measure. |
| **Opt-out rate** | — | Share of users opting out of tracking; a confound when reading Analytics (a high opt-out can invalidate "traffic is up" claims). |

> [!warning] Analytics-scenario trap
> "Page Visits are higher → campaign is successful" is **not** sound: a high **opt-out rate**, low **CTR**, or low **time on site** can all undercut it. These appear as scenario MCQs (2024-A Q12, 2024-B Q17).

## Channels, platforms & media

| Acronym | Stands for | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **POEM** | Paid, Owned, Earned Media | The cross-platform media framework; planning across all three is the goal. |
| **GDN** | Google Display Network | Google's display/banner network across publisher sites; reaches audiences **not active on social media**; gives publishers more certain ad revenue. |
| **SEM** | Search Engine Marketing | Paid search advertising. **⚠ collides with "Structural Equation Modelling" (research method) — context matters.** |
| **SEO** | Search Engine Optimization | Organic improvement of search ranking. |
| **SERP** | Search Engine Results Page | The page returned for a query. |
| **UGC** | User Generated Content | Brand-relevant content created by users/consumers. |
| **FAST** | Free Ad-supported Streaming TV | Ad-funded streaming channels (vs subscription/linear TV). |

> [!info] Linear viewing (term, not acronym)
> **Linear viewing** = watching content **at the time of its original broadcast/distribution as set by the publisher** (e.g. live TV) — contrast with on-demand. (Defined in past exams.)

## Strategy & branding models

| Acronym | Stands for | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **STP** | Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning | Core strategy sequence; positioning is the "P". |
| **USP / UVP** | Unique Selling / Value Proposition | The distinctive benefit/value a brand offers. |
| **4Ps** | Product, Price, Place, Promotion | Classic (firm-centric) marketing mix. |
| **4Cs** | Consumer/Customer, Cost, Convenience, Communication | Customer-centric reframing of the 4Ps (shift = product→customer centricity). |
| **CRM** | Customer Relationship Management | Systems/strategy for managing customer relationships and data. |
| **CDJ** | Customer Decision Journey | Loyalty-loop model replacing the linear sales funnel (esp. B2B — Dell). |
| **CBBE** | Customer-Based Brand Equity | Keller's model of brand equity from the consumer's perspective. (Ebrahim) |
| **STEPPS** | Social currency, Triggers, Emotion, Public, Practical value, Stories | Berger's *Contagious* virality model. |
| **AIDA** | Awareness, Interest, Desire, Action | Classic purchase-funnel stages. |
| **B2B / B2C** | Business-to-Business / -Consumer | Market type; B2B has derived demand, DMUs, longer cycles. |
| **DMU** | Decision-Making Unit | The group of people involved in a B2B purchase decision. |
| **CSR** | Corporate Social Responsibility | Corporate accountability for social/environmental impact. (BrandFusion §3.13) |

## Customer engagement value (Kumar et al.)

| Acronym | Stands for | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **CE** | Customer Engagement | Umbrella construct of a customer's total value contribution. |
| **CLV** | Customer Lifetime Value | Value from a customer's own purchases over time. |
| **CRV** | Customer Referral Value | Value from customers they refer. |
| **CIV** | Customer Influence Value | Value from their social influence/word-of-mouth. |
| **CKV** | Customer Knowledge Value | Value from the feedback/knowledge they provide (key in an AI world). |

## AI, GenAI & advertising tech

| Acronym | Stands for | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **AI** | Artificial Intelligence | Learning systems acting via flexible adaptation. |
| **GenAI / GAI** | Generative AI | AI that creates **novel + appropriate** content (text, image, audio, video). |
| **LLM** | Large Language Model | A large pre-trained language **foundation model**. |
| **ML** | Machine Learning | The learning building block of AI. |
| **GAN** | Generative Adversarial Network | Generator + discriminator that **fabricate new** media (Campbell). |
| **Narrow / General / Super AI** | — | Rodgers' AI-type axis (single-task → human-level → beyond-human). |

## Research-method acronyms (appear in "according to the study…" questions)

| Acronym | Stands for | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **SEM** | Structural Equation Modelling | Multivariate technique testing mediation/paths (Ebrahim; Lee & Theokary use **PLS-SEM**). ⚠ Same letters as Search Engine Marketing. |
| **TPB** | Theory of Planned Behaviour | Ajzen — intentions drive behaviour (Kariv). |
| **EEM** | Entrepreneurial Event Model | Shapero & Sokol — feasibility + desirability drive intentions (Kariv). |
| **ELM** | Elaboration Likelihood Model | Dual-route (central/peripheral) persuasion (Lee & Theokary). |
| **LET** | Language Expectancy Theory | Language-style expectation violations alter persuasion (Lee & Theokary). |
| **ECT** | Emotional Contagion Theory | People "catch" displayed emotions (Lee & Theokary). |
| **PKM** | Persuasion Knowledge Model | How consumers recognise/cope with persuasion attempts (contrast in Lee & Theokary; Campbell). |
| **MTA** | Multi-Touch Attribution | Distributes conversion credit across touchpoints (Buhalis & Volchek). |
