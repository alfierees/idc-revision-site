## Topic 1 — Marketing 101 & Shift to Digital

### [[01-Kariv - Embedding the Marketing Angle in Entrepreneurial Propensity|Kariv et al. (2025)]]
**Thesis:** What turns entrepreneurial *intention* into *action* is the marketing "content" of feasibility and desirability — i.e. perceived **marketing capabilities** — plus the right stakeholder support.

- **Marketing capabilities** (Vorhies & Morgan): market information management, marketing communications, market planning, product development.
- **Feasibility = "can I?"** (self-efficacy); **Desirability = "do I want to?"** (attractiveness). Built on **TPB** (Ajzen) + **EEM** (Shapero & Sokol).
- Stakeholder support 2×2: **bridging vs buffering** × **people vs institutions**.
- Core finding: marketing/psychological *content* separates **action from non-action**; stakeholder support separates **intenders from non-intenders**. Mediation MF→MD→propensity.
- Terms/acronyms: **TPB, EEM**, Market Feasibility (MF), Market Desirability (MD).

> Exam angle: definition of marketing capabilities; feasibility vs desirability; what distinguishes "doers" from "intenders".

## Topic 2 — Media Strategy, Platforms & Channels

### [[02a-Buhalis & Volchek - Taxonomy of Marketing Attribution|Buhalis & Volchek (2021)]]
**Thesis:** A **five-dimensional taxonomy** to consistently name any marketing-attribution method; even "advanced" methods still miss the *context* of decision-making.

- **Five dimensions:** (1) single vs multi-touch, (2) fractional vs incremental, (3) cross-digital/platform/channel, (4) rule-based vs data-driven, (5) standardised vs custom.
- First-click / last-click = simplest **single-touch**; **U-shape** & **time-decay** = classic rule-based weightings.
- Single-touch is biased on **long** journeys (ignores timing, sequence, causality).
- Key limitation: no method models the **context** of decision-making.
- Terms/acronyms: **attribution, touchpoint, customer journey, multi-touch attribution (MTA), data-driven attribution**.

> Exam angle: match a weighting rule (U-shape/time-decay/last-click) to its description; the five dimensions; why single-touch fails.

### [[02b-Moorman et al - Why Marketers Are Returning to Traditional Advertising|Moorman, Ryan & Tavassoli (2022)]]
**Thesis (HBR):** Traditional advertising is rebounding; the future is traditional **+** digital together, not one vs the other.

- **Seven drivers:** (1) breaks through **digital clutter**, (2) builds **trust**, (3) **third-party cookie** decline, (4) rise of **podcasting**, (5) traditional **lifts digital** performance, (6) **brand-building fit**, (7) scepticism about digital effectiveness/fraud.
- Source = **The CMO Survey** (Christine Moorman, Duke). Reversal led by **digital-native** firms (+11.7%).
- Terms/acronyms: ad fatigue, digital clutter, **ROI**, ad fraud, **CMO**.

> Exam angle (✓ directly tested in 2024-A): *which factor explains the resurgence of traditional advertising?* → ad fatigue / breaking through clutter.

## Topic 3 — Formation of Campaign Strategy

### [[03a-Hong et al - Sharing Consumers Brand Storytelling|Hong et al. (2021)]]
**Thesis:** A brand story told **by consumers** beats one told **by the firm** on brand attitude, via a **dual mechanism** (cognition + emotion).

- Three constructs: **brand story** (firm, controlled, one-to-many) vs **consumers' brand storytelling** (consumer-created) vs **WoM** (broader, not necessarily a narrative).
- Dual mediation: consumer storytelling → deeper **cognitive processing** AND stronger **positive emotions** → better attitude.
- Why it works: similarity → **self-referencing**, **narrative transportation**, perceived authenticity, less bias, more shareable.
- Method: 2×2 between-subjects experiments + PROCESS mediation.
- Terms/acronyms: **WoM**, narrative transportation, self-referencing, **CGC/UGC**.

> Exam angle: consumer vs firm storytelling; the dual cognitive+emotional mediation; advantage of UGC-style storytelling.

### [[03b-BrandFusion Ch4 - Purpose, Proposition & Positioning|BrandFusion Ch4]]
**Thesis:** Durable strategy aligns **purpose** (why), **value proposition** (promise), and **positioning** (place in the mind); the shift is from shareholder primacy to stakeholder capitalism.

- Distinguish all five: **Purpose = why · Mission = what (now) · Vision = where (future) · Value proposition = promise · Positioning = place in the mind.**
- **Shareholder primacy** (Friedman) → **stakeholder capitalism** ("shared value", Porter & Kramer).
- **Positioning statement** = target + frame of reference + point of difference + reason to believe.
- Purpose pays: Stengel 50, Cone stats, Unilever Sustainable Living brands. Sinek "people buy *why*"; Drucker profit = oxygen.
- Terms/acronyms: **STP, USP/UVP**, similarity vs typicality.

> Exam angle: distinguish purpose/mission/vision/positioning; shareholder vs stakeholder; identify a positioning-statement component.

## Topic 4 — Brand Identity & Storytelling

### [[04a-Salzer-Morling & Strannegard - Silence of the Brands|Salzer-Mörling & Strannegård (2004)]]
**Thesis:** Brands are **silent** — they *show* through images (feeling, immersion), not *tell* through narratives; meaning is **co-constructed** by consumers, not owned by the firm.

- Contrast the **sender→message→receiver** model with the **double-agency / co-construction** view.
- Brands communicate via **figural** images (sensation) not **discursive** stories (meaning/persuasion).
- Examples: **Fred Perry / skinheads** (uncontrolled appropriation); **adbusters / culture jamming** (subversion).
- Aaker's four brand-equity assets used as the model being critiqued. "Meaning is an **effect**, not a starting point."
- Terms/acronyms: **brandscape, mediascape, aesthetic reflexivity, brand equity**.

> Exam angle: define brandscape; "silent" brands thesis; meaning as co-created vs firm-controlled.

### [[04b-Aime - The Dynamic Construction of Brand Storytelling|Aimé (2021)]]
**Thesis:** Brand storytelling is **not** a single unified message — internal stakeholders each *perform* their own version, and digital touchpoints fragment it; co-creation extends *inside* the firm.

- Case: **Petit Bateau**, 18 interviews → internal "schizophrenia" / **polysemous brand**.
- **Brand performativity** exists within brand management itself.
- Digital ecosystem multiplies touchpoints → threatens **consistency**.
- 3-step framework: write the story → common strategy → channel synergy. Manager as **conductor**; **react, not control**.
- Terms/acronyms: co-creation, brand performativity, 360° content.

> Exam angle (✓ tested 2024-A Q9): the *foundational practice* for long-term co-creation → strong brand personalities + adaptable story universe.

## Topic 5 — Content Marketing

### [[05-Ebrahim - Trust, Social Media Marketing, Brand Equity & Loyalty|Ebrahim (2020)]]
**Thesis:** SMM drives brand equity and loyalty **mainly through brand trust** — trust is the focal mediator.

- Causal chain: **SMM → Brand Trust → Brand Equity / Brand Loyalty**.
- **Full mediation** of SMM→equity by trust; **partial mediation** of SMM→loyalty; **no direct** SMM→equity.
- Five SMM dimensions (entertainment, interaction, **trendiness, customisation, WOM**) — but only the last three loaded here.
- Method: online survey, n = 287, Egypt telecom, **SEM** (structural equation modelling). Social media = **pull**, vs traditional **interruption**.
- Terms/acronyms: **SMM, CBBE** (customer-based brand equity), behavioural vs attitudinal loyalty.

> Exam angle (✓ tested 2024-B Q12): what is *crucial* to SMM's impact on equity/loyalty → the role of **trust**.

## Topic 6 — Marketing Challenges / Startups

### [[06a-Cillo & Rubera - Generative AI in Innovation & Marketing Processes|Cillo & Rubera (2024)]]
**Thesis:** GenAI's distinctive power is creating **novel + appropriate** content; mapped across a four-stage innovation process, it may both empower and erode marketing's value contribution.

- GenAI = **"feeling/creating" AI**, distinct from mechanical and thinking AI. Self-supervised learning + Transformer + scale → **foundation models**.
- Creativity = novel **and** appropriate; limits = **hallucinations**, weak causal reasoning.
- Four-stage circular **innovation process**: developing → testing → communicating → engaging.
- Tensions: AI-conformity vs diversification; **algorithm aversion** vs disclosure; psychological ownership vs reach.
- Terms/acronyms: **GenAI/GAI, LLM**, foundation model, market-based assets, algorithm aversion.

> Exam angle: what makes GenAI different (novel+appropriate); the four innovation stages; algorithm aversion.

### [[06b-BrandFusion Ch17 - Dell Technologies Person-to-Person B2B|BrandFusion Ch17 (Dell)]]
**Thesis:** Corporate brand identity and **personality** create value even in rational **B2B**, by balancing functional (tangible) and emotional (intangible) value.

- B2B specifics: **derived demand**, concentrated buyers, **DMUs**, longer pathway — branding still creates value.
- **Branding Triangle** = customer + company + collaboration → customer-company fusion.
- Value exploration → creation → delivery (Kotler & Pföertsch). **CDJ / loyalty loop** replaces the linear funnel.
- Case: Dell ↔ BP, ~100k users; ~60% / ~$30m saving per refresh cycle. Founder personality (Michael Dell).
- Terms/acronyms: **B2B, DMU, CDJ**, tangible vs intangible value.

> Exam angle: B2B branding characteristics; tangible vs intangible value; CDJ vs sales funnel.

## Topic 7 — Trends: Mobile / Voice / Location

### [[07a-GenAI in Marketing - Applications, Opportunities & Research Agenda|GenAI in Marketing (2024)]]
**Thesis:** Marketing is the function most positively transformed by GAI; top uses are **personalization, insight generation, content creation**; GAI augments rather than replaces humans.

- **Top three applications** (BCG 2023): personalization, insight generation, content creation.
- Facilitators: ease of use, trialability, low cost, tool variety, fine-tuning. Barriers: cost for SMEs, **data privacy/security**, transition cost, culture, job-loss fears.
- Five propositions → more personally relevant content + higher efficiency/productivity + better **lead generation**.
- Personalization at scale needs **CRM**/marketing-automation integration.
- Stats: 73% US adoption (Mar 2023); ~5 hrs/week saved; 5–15% spend saving (McKinsey).
- Terms/acronyms: **GAI, CRM, BCG, SME**.

> Exam angle: name the top-three GAI applications; facilitators vs barriers; augments-not-replaces.

### [[07b-Iglesias et al - Corporate Brand Identity Co-Creation in B2B|Iglesias et al. (2020)]]
**Thesis:** Corporate brand identity is **not** fixed/top-down — it's fluid, polysemous and **co-created** via four stakeholder performances; managers shift from guardian to conductor.

- Traditional (stable, top-down) vs emerging (dynamic, multi-stakeholder co-created) view.
- **Four performances: communicating, internalizing, contesting, elucidating** (define each).
- Identity *stems from founders' values* but is a **temporary outcome** of ongoing co-creation.
- Manager role: **brand custodian/guardian → brand conductor**.
- Method: multiple-case study, 5 B2B SMEs, 37 narrative interviews (performativity / Goffman lens).
- Terms/acronyms: corporate brand identity, co-creation, **B2B, SME**, boundary spanners.

> Exam angle: the four stakeholder performances; guardian→conductor shift; dynamic vs static identity.

## Topic 8 — Deepfakes & Manipulated Advertising

### [[08-Campbell et al - Preparing for an Era of Deepfakes & AI-Generated Ads|Campbell et al. (2022)]]
**Thesis:** Ads are moving to **synthetic** (AI-generated) form; sophistication raises **verisimilitude** & **creativity** (boost persuasion) but can trigger **awareness of falsity** (dampens it).

- Three generations: **analog → digital → synthetic** (know examples + what defines each).
- **Deepfakes** (autoencoders, *edit* existing media) vs **GANs** (generator/discriminator, *generate* new media).
- Three mechanisms: **verisimilitude, creativity, awareness of falsity** (falsity sits at the centre).
- Hyperpersonalisation + covert **customer surveillance** → privacy concerns, reactance ("advertising calculus").
- Terms/acronyms: **GAN**, deepfake, synthetic advertising, verisimilitude, persuasion knowledge.

> Exam angle: deepfakes vs GANs; the analog/digital/synthetic spectrum; verisimilitude definition.

## Topic 9 — Same Content Diff. Platforms / AI in Advertising

### [[09a-Rodgers - Promises and Perils of AI and Advertising|Rodgers (2021)]]
**Thesis (editorial):** Defines **AI advertising** and gives a classification schema; frames the field as promises vs perils, with **algorithm bias/transparency** as the central concern.

- Definition: "brand communication using machine functions that **learn** to carry out tasks with **intent to persuade**, with human/machine input."
- Schema: **AI type** (narrow/general/super) × **AI function** × **learning type**.
- "Promises and perils" framing; examples incl. **AI influencers**.
- Closing concern: **algorithm bias + algorithmic transparency**; AI is *not* value-neutral.
- Terms/acronyms: **AI**, narrow/general/super AI, **ML**, algorithmic transparency.

> Exam angle: the AI-advertising definition; narrow vs general AI; transparency/bias as the key peril.

### [[09b-Lee & Theokary - The Superstar Social Media Influencer|Lee & Theokary (2021)]]
**Thesis:** For influencers the persuasion routes **reverse**: linguistic style + emotion become **central**; content/production **expertise** becomes peripheral.

- Theories: **ELM** (framework) + **LET** (linguistic style) + **ECT** (emotional contagion); contrasts the **PKM**.
- **Reversal of the ELM**: style is central, expertise peripheral (a mediator only).
- Four linguistic-style dimensions — **preciseness is NOT significant**.
- **Emotional contagion has no direct effect** — fully mediated by expertise.
- Method: 27 YouTube auto channels, speech-to-text + survey + Social Blade, **PLS-SEM**.
- Terms/acronyms: **ELM, LET, ECT, PKM, SEM**.

> Exam angle: what drives influencer success (style/emotion, not expertise); the ELM reversal; theories used.

## Topic 10 — Optimization, Data & Measurement

### [[10-Kumar et al - AI in Personalized Engagement Marketing|Kumar et al. (2019)]]
**Thesis:** AI enables **personalized engagement marketing** via **curation**; consumers are in a "Wave 3" journey; predictions vary by time and economy.

- **Personalization (firm-controlled) vs Customization (customer-controlled)** — know the difference.
- **Three waves**; Wave 3 emerges from the **paradox of choice** / digital cognitive load. **Curation** = machine-driven selection of the mix.
- Four CE dimensions: **CLV, CRV, CIV, CKV** (CKV matters more in an AI world).
- Predictions across **time (short/long) × place (developed/developing)** over the three CRM pillars (acquisition, retention, growth).
- Retention challenges: **uncanny valley**, **algorithm aversion**.
- Terms/acronyms: **CLV, CRV, CIV, CKV, CE, CRM, AI**.

> Exam angle: personalization vs customization; define curation; the CLV/CRV/CIV/CKV set; uncanny valley.

## Topic 11 — Social & Public Campaigns / CSR

### [[11-BrandFusion Ch3.13 - Social Conscience & Social Marketing|BrandFusion §3.13]]
**Thesis:** Brand strategy is increasingly **socially driven**; **social marketing** applies marketing techniques to achieve **behaviour change** for social good.

- Definition: *systematic application of marketing concepts/techniques to achieve specific **behavioural** goals for a social/public good* — core = **behaviour change**.
- Intervention levels: **upstream (policy) / midstream (community) / downstream (individual)**.
- Six core social-marketing concepts (esp. explicit goals + metrics, citizen orientation, competition/barrier analysis).
- "The meaning of marketing is the marketing of meaning"; shareholder → **social value**. Social conscience = 1 of 8 elements of **Social-Dominant Logic**.
- Cases: **Dove, Always #LikeAGirl, Libresse #BloodNormal, TOMS, Absolut**; also the **Fearless Girl** style move.
- Terms/acronyms: **CSR**, social marketing, social-dominant logic, purpose-driven branding.

> Exam angle (✓ scenario style — cf. the "Fearless Girl" example in the exam doc): match a purpose-led PR stunt to social marketing / cross-platform POEM; define social marketing; upstream/mid/downstream.
