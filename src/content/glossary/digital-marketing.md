## Marketing strategy & entrepreneurship

### Marketing Capabilities
A firm's market-sensing, communications, marketing-planning and product-development competences. → [[01-Kariv - Embedding the Marketing Angle in Entrepreneurial Propensity|Kariv]]

### Entrepreneurial Propensity
The disposition to convert entrepreneurial intentions into the act of starting a venture. → [[01-Kariv - Embedding the Marketing Angle in Entrepreneurial Propensity|Kariv]]

### Perceived Feasibility
Belief that one has the skills/resources to start a venture (self-efficacy). → [[01-Kariv - Embedding the Marketing Angle in Entrepreneurial Propensity|Kariv]]

### Perceived Desirability
Belief that the venture's outcome is personally and socially attractive. → [[01-Kariv - Embedding the Marketing Angle in Entrepreneurial Propensity|Kariv]]

### Stakeholder Support
External help (financial, informational, emotional) classed by type and function. → [[01-Kariv - Embedding the Marketing Angle in Entrepreneurial Propensity|Kariv]]

### Theory of Planned Behaviour
Ajzen's model: intentions (attitude, norms, control) drive behaviour. → [[01-Kariv - Embedding the Marketing Angle in Entrepreneurial Propensity|Kariv]]

### Entrepreneurial Event Model
Shapero & Sokol: intentions arise from perceived feasibility + desirability. → [[01-Kariv - Embedding the Marketing Angle in Entrepreneurial Propensity|Kariv]]

### Marketing Mix
The controllable parameters (e.g. 4Ps) shaping each touchpoint's effect. → [[02a-Buhalis & Volchek - Taxonomy of Marketing Attribution|Buhalis & Volchek]]

### Segmentation Targeting Positioning
The STP framework; positioning is the strategic "P". → [[03b-BrandFusion Ch4 - Purpose, Proposition & Positioning|BrandFusion Ch4]]

### Strategic Positioning
Occupying a distinct, valued place in the prospect's mind. → [[03b-BrandFusion Ch4 - Purpose, Proposition & Positioning|BrandFusion Ch4]]

### Value Proposition
The functional, emotional and self-expressive benefits a customer perceives. → [[03b-BrandFusion Ch4 - Purpose, Proposition & Positioning|BrandFusion Ch4]]

### Vision Statement
The aspirational future destination ("where you are going"). → [[03b-BrandFusion Ch4 - Purpose, Proposition & Positioning|BrandFusion Ch4]]

### Mission Statement
What the organisation does now, for whom, and how. → [[03b-BrandFusion Ch4 - Purpose, Proposition & Positioning|BrandFusion Ch4]]

## Attribution & measurement

### Marketing Attribution
Allocating conversion value across the touchpoints of a customer journey. → [[02a-Buhalis & Volchek - Taxonomy of Marketing Attribution|Buhalis & Volchek]]

### Customer Journey
The sequence of brand interactions a customer has before converting. → [[02a-Buhalis & Volchek - Taxonomy of Marketing Attribution|Buhalis & Volchek]]

### Touchpoint
A single customer–brand interaction occurring via a channel. → [[02a-Buhalis & Volchek - Taxonomy of Marketing Attribution|Buhalis & Volchek]]

### Multi-Touch Attribution
Distributes conversion credit across several touchpoints rather than one. → [[02a-Buhalis & Volchek - Taxonomy of Marketing Attribution|Buhalis & Volchek]]

### Data-Driven Attribution
Empirically derives each touchpoint's weight from observed data. → [[02a-Buhalis & Volchek - Taxonomy of Marketing Attribution|Buhalis & Volchek]]

### Purchase Funnel
Awareness → interest → desire → action stages toward conversion. → [[02a-Buhalis & Volchek - Taxonomy of Marketing Attribution|Buhalis & Volchek]]

### Customer Engagement
A customer's value via CLV, CRV, CIV and CKV contributions. → [[10-Kumar et al - AI in Personalized Engagement Marketing|Kumar]]

## Media & advertising channels

### Digital Clutter

## Brand identity & storytelling

### Brand Identity
The name/logo/design whole positioned at the centre of brand theory. → [[04a-Salzer-Morling & Strannegard - Silence of the Brands|Silence of Brands]]

### Brand Storytelling
A compelling brand narrative that builds emotional connection. → [[03a-Hong et al - Sharing Consumers Brand Storytelling|Hong]]

### Brand Story
A firm-created, controlled, one-to-many brand narrative. → [[03a-Hong et al - Sharing Consumers Brand Storytelling|Hong]]

### Brand Attitude
A consumer's combined rational + emotional evaluation of a brand. → [[03a-Hong et al - Sharing Consumers Brand Storytelling|Hong]]

### Narrative Transportation
The state of being mentally absorbed into a story. → [[03a-Hong et al - Sharing Consumers Brand Storytelling|Hong]]

### Word of Mouth
Informal consumer-to-consumer brand communication. → [[03a-Hong et al - Sharing Consumers Brand Storytelling|Hong]]

Brand-relevant content created by consumers rather than the firm. → [[03a-Hong et al - Sharing Consumers Brand Storytelling|Hong]]

### Brandscape
The commercial landscape where brand icons are produced and consumed. → [[04a-Salzer-Morling & Strannegard - Silence of the Brands|Silence of Brands]]

### Brand Performativity

### Brand Purpose

## Media buying & pricing models

### CPM
Cost Per Mille — Cost per **1,000 impressions**; you pay for exposure/reach (low price, low engagement guarantee).

### CPC
Cost Per Click — You pay only when a user **clicks** the ad.

### CPA
Cost Per Acquisition / Action — You pay only when a defined **action/conversion** occurs (low pricing, high engagement requirement).

### CPL
Cost Per Lead — You pay per qualified **lead** captured.

### CPV
Cost Per View — You pay per **video view**.

### PPC
Pay Per Click — Pricing/bidding model where advertiser pays per click (e.g. Google Ads).

## Performance metrics & KPIs

### KPI
Key Performance Indicator — A metric chosen to track progress toward an objective.

### OMTM
One Metric That Matters — The single most important metric to focus on at a given stage.

### CTR
Click-Through Rate — Clicks ÷ impressions; ad engagement measure.

### CR
Conversion Rate — Conversions ÷ visitors (or clicks).

### ROI
Return On Investment — Net return relative to cost.

### ROAS
Return On Ad Spend — Revenue generated per unit of ad spend.

### MAU / DAU
Monthly / Daily Active Users — Count of unique active users in the period; engagement/retention measure.

### Opt-out rate
Share of users opting out of tracking; a confound when reading Analytics (a high opt-out can invalidate "traffic is up" claims).

## Strategy & branding models

### STP
Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning — Core strategy sequence; positioning is the "P".

### USP / UVP
Unique Selling / Value Proposition — The distinctive benefit/value a brand offers.

### 4Ps
Product, Price, Place, Promotion — Classic (firm-centric) marketing mix.

### 4Cs
Consumer/Customer, Cost, Convenience, Communication — Customer-centric reframing of the 4Ps (shift = product→customer centricity).

### CRM
Customer Relationship Management — Systems/strategy for managing customer relationships and data.

### CDJ
Customer Decision Journey — Loyalty-loop model replacing the linear sales funnel (esp. B2B — Dell).

### CBBE
Customer-Based Brand Equity — Keller's model of brand equity from the consumer's perspective. (Ebrahim)

### STEPPS
Social currency, Triggers, Emotion, Public, Practical value, Stories — Berger's *Contagious* virality model. **[deck]**

### AIDA
Awareness, Interest, Desire, Action — Classic purchase-funnel stages. **[deck]**

### B2B / B2C
Business-to-Business / -Consumer — Market type; B2B has derived demand, DMUs, longer cycles.

### DMU
Decision-Making Unit — The group of people involved in a B2B purchase decision.

### CSR
Corporate Social Responsibility — Corporate accountability for social/environmental impact. (BrandFusion §3.13)

## Customer engagement value (Kumar et al.)

### CE
Customer Engagement — Umbrella construct of a customer's total value contribution.

### CLV
Customer Lifetime Value — Value from a customer's own purchases over time.

### CRV
Customer Referral Value — Value from customers they refer.

### CIV
Customer Influence Value — Value from their social influence/word-of-mouth.

### CKV
Customer Knowledge Value — Value from the feedback/knowledge they provide (key in an AI world).

## AI, GenAI & advertising tech

### AI
Artificial Intelligence — Learning systems acting via flexible adaptation.

### GenAI / GAI
Generative AI — AI that creates **novel + appropriate** content (text, image, audio, video).

### LLM
Large Language Model — A large pre-trained language **foundation model**.

### ML
Machine Learning — The learning building block of AI.

### GAN
Generative Adversarial Network — Generator + discriminator that **fabricate new** media (Campbell).

### Narrow / General / Super AI
Rodgers' AI-type axis (single-task → human-level → beyond-human).

### EEM

### ECT

### PKM
