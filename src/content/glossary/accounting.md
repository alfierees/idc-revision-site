## Financial statements & fundamentals

### Financial Statements
The five interconnected reports that summarise a company's financial position and performance. → [[Session 2 - Accounting Fundamentals|Session 2]]

### Balance Sheet
Statement of financial position at a point in time: Assets = Liabilities + Equity. → [[Session 2 - Accounting Fundamentals|Session 2]]

### Income Statement
The P&L: Revenue − Expenses = Profit / Loss over a period. → [[Session 2 - Accounting Fundamentals|Session 2]]

### Cash Flows Statement
Reconciles profit to cash, split into operating, investing, and financing activities. → [[Session 2 - Accounting Fundamentals|Session 2]]

### Changes in Shareholders' Equity
Statement of how owners' stake changed over the period (issuance, dividends, retained earnings). → [[Session 2 - Accounting Fundamentals|Session 2]]

### Double Entry Bookkeeping
Every transaction touches at least two accounts so that total debits = total credits. → [[Session 2 - Accounting Fundamentals|Session 2]]

### Accrual Accounting
Record revenues and expenses when the economic event occurs, not when cash moves. → [[Session 2 - Accounting Fundamentals|Session 2]]

### Matching Principle
Match expenses to the revenue they help generate in the same period. → [[Session 2 - Accounting Fundamentals|Session 2]]

### Depreciation
Spreading the cost of a long-lived asset over its useful life. → [[Session 2 - Accounting Fundamentals|Session 2]]

### Cut-Off Accounts
Period-end adjustments aligning revenues and expenses to the correct period. → [[Session 2 - Accounting Fundamentals|Session 2]]

### Prepaid Expenses
Cut-off asset: cash paid in advance of consuming the benefit. → [[Session 2 - Accounting Fundamentals|Session 2]]

### Income Receivable
Cut-off asset: income earned but cash received late. In revenue recognition, the **contract asset** — a conditional right to consideration. → [[Session 2 - Accounting Fundamentals|Session 2]]

### Accrued Expenses
Cut-off liability: expense incurred but cash paid late. → [[Session 2 - Accounting Fundamentals|Session 2]]

### Deferred Income
Cut-off liability: cash received early, before the good/service is delivered. → [[Session 2 - Accounting Fundamentals|Session 2]]

## Raising debt & capital

### Capital Structure
The mix of liabilities and equity funding the company. → [[Session 3 - Raising Debt and Capital|Session 3]]

### Leverage
A high-debt capital structure; raises both financial risk and the [[Tax Shield]] benefit. → [[Session 3 - Raising Debt and Capital|Session 3]]

### Common Stock
Ordinary shares carrying voting, dividend, and dissolution rights. → [[Session 3 - Raising Debt and Capital|Session 3]]

### Preferred Stock
Shares with negotiated preferences (e.g. liquidation, dividends) over [[Common Stock]]. → [[Session 3 - Raising Debt and Capital|Session 3]]

### SAFE
Simple Agreement for Future Equity — an early-stage instrument converting to equity at a later priced round. → [[Session 3 - Raising Debt and Capital|Session 3]]

### Pre-Money Valuation
The company's value *before* a new investment. → [[Session 3 - Raising Debt and Capital|Session 3]]

### Post-Money Valuation
The company's value immediately *after* a new investment (pre-money + amount raised). → [[Session 3 - Raising Debt and Capital|Session 3]]

### Tax Shield
The tax saving from deducting interest expense against taxable profit. → [[Session 3 - Raising Debt and Capital|Session 3]]

### Convertible Bonds
Debt that may convert into equity under agreed terms. → [[Session 3 - Raising Debt and Capital|Session 3]]

## Incorporation, taxes & employee compensation

### Capital Offering
Issuing securities to investors in exchange for cash. → [[Session 4 - Incorporation, Corporate Taxes & Employee Compensation|Session 4]]

### Share Purchase Agreement (SPA)
The private contract governing a capital raise. → [[Session 4 - Incorporation, Corporate Taxes & Employee Compensation|Session 4]]

### Liquidation Preference
Preferred shareholders' right to be paid first in an exit. → [[Session 4 - Incorporation, Corporate Taxes & Employee Compensation|Session 4]]

### Anti-Dilution
Protection that adjusts the preferred-share conversion ratio in a down round. → [[Session 4 - Incorporation, Corporate Taxes & Employee Compensation|Session 4]]

### IPO
Initial Public Offering — the first sale of shares to the public. → [[Session 4 - Incorporation, Corporate Taxes & Employee Compensation|Session 4]]

### Corporate Tax
Tax paid by a corporation on its profits. → [[Session 4 - Incorporation, Corporate Taxes & Employee Compensation|Session 4]]

### Transfer Pricing
Rules governing the prices charged between related entities. → [[Session 4 - Incorporation, Corporate Taxes & Employee Compensation|Session 4]]

### GILTI
US tax on the low-taxed foreign income of US-owned multinationals. → [[Session 4 - Incorporation, Corporate Taxes & Employee Compensation|Session 4]]

### FDII
US tax incentive for keeping IP in the US and selling internationally. → [[Session 4 - Incorporation, Corporate Taxes & Employee Compensation|Session 4]]

### Payroll
All cash compensation — salary, bonus, vacation, severance, pension. → [[Session 4 - Incorporation, Corporate Taxes & Employee Compensation|Session 4]]

### Stock Options
The right (not obligation) to buy company shares at a fixed exercise price. → [[Session 4 - Incorporation, Corporate Taxes & Employee Compensation|Session 4]]

### Vesting
Gradual earning of the right to exercise options (or receive shares) over time. → [[Session 4 - Incorporation, Corporate Taxes & Employee Compensation|Session 4]]

### Black-Scholes Model
A mathematical model for valuing options. → [[Session 4 - Incorporation, Corporate Taxes & Employee Compensation|Session 4]]

### RSU
Restricted Stock Unit — shares granted after [[Vesting]], with no exercise price. → [[Session 4 - Incorporation, Corporate Taxes & Employee Compensation|Session 4]]

### Section 102
Israeli tax law governing the taxation of employee stock options. → [[Session 4 - Incorporation, Corporate Taxes & Employee Compensation|Session 4]]

## Financial statement analysis

### Horizontal Analysis
Year-on-year comparison of a company's performance. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### Vertical Analysis
Expressing each line as a % of revenue to compare across companies/periods. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### Ratio Analysis
Standardised financial ratios for comparing efficiency and health. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### Current Ratio
Current assets ÷ current liabilities — short-term liquidity. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### Quick Ratio
Liquid assets only ÷ current liabilities — a stricter liquidity test. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### Working Capital
Current assets minus current liabilities — the absolute liquidity buffer. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### Gross Profit Margin
Gross profit ÷ revenue — core operational efficiency. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### EBITDA
Earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation and amortization. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### ROA
Net income ÷ average total assets — asset efficiency. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### ROE
Net income ÷ average equity — return to shareholders. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### ROCE
EBIT ÷ capital employed; must exceed [[WACC]] to create value. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### Rule of 40
SaaS health metric: revenue growth % + EBITDA margin % ≥ 40. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### MRR
Monthly Recurring Revenue — total predictable monthly subscription revenue. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### NRR
Net Revenue Retention — revenue retained/expanded from existing customers. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### Churn Rate
Revenue lost from cancellations as a % of total [[MRR]]. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### CAC
Customer Acquisition Cost — total sales & marketing spend per new customer. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### LTV
Lifetime Value — total revenue expected from a customer over their lifetime. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

### DCF Model
Discounted Cash Flow — projects future cash flows and discounts them to NPV. → [[Session 5 - Financial Statements Analysis|Session 5]]

## Financial planning & analysis

### Budget
A quantitative plan for a period expressing expected outputs, inputs, and resources. → [[Session 6-Financial Planning and Analysis|Session 6]]

### Standard Costing
A system of predetermined cost targets (price standard × quantity standard). → [[Session 6-Financial Planning and Analysis|Session 6]]

### Variance Analysis
Decomposing the gap between standard and actual costs. → [[Session 6-Financial Planning and Analysis|Session 6]]

### Price Variance
The portion of cost variance from paying more/less per unit than standard. → [[Session 6-Financial Planning and Analysis|Session 6]]

### Quantity Variance
The portion of cost variance from using more/less than the standard quantity. → [[Session 6-Financial Planning and Analysis|Session 6]]

### Budget Variance
Overhead price variance — actual overhead prices differ from forecast. → [[Session 6-Financial Planning and Analysis|Session 6]]

### Volume Variance
Overhead quantity variance — actual output differs from forecast. → [[Session 6-Financial Planning and Analysis|Session 6]]

### Responsibility Centers
Organisational units with defined financial accountability. → [[Session 6-Financial Planning and Analysis|Session 6]]

### Revenue Center
A unit whose manager is accountable for revenue only. → [[Session 6-Financial Planning and Analysis|Session 6]]

### Cost Center
A unit whose manager is accountable for costs only. → [[Session 6-Financial Planning and Analysis|Session 6]]

### Profit Center
A unit whose manager is accountable for both revenue and costs. → [[Session 6-Financial Planning and Analysis|Session 6]]

### Investment Center
A unit whose manager is accountable for investments and [[ROA]]. → [[Session 6-Financial Planning and Analysis|Session 6]]

## Business combinations & consolidation

### Business Combination
A transaction where an acquiror obtains control over an acquiree; requires full [[Consolidation]]. → [[Session 7 - Business Combination|Session 7]]

### Financial Asset
< 20% ownership — recorded at cost, then fair value through P&L. → [[Session 7 - Business Combination|Session 7]]

### Associated Company
20–50% ownership — accounted under the [[Equity Method]]. → [[Session 7 - Business Combination|Session 7]]

### Equity Method
Share of the associate's profit is added to the investment; dividends reduce it. → [[Session 7 - Business Combination|Session 7]]

### Acquisition Method
The only permitted method for business combinations under IFRS 3. → [[Session 7 - Business Combination|Session 7]]

### Goodwill
Residual of consideration + NCI fair value minus net identifiable assets at fair value. → [[Session 7 - Business Combination|Session 7]]

### Purchase Price Allocation
PPA — allocating the consideration to specific assets/liabilities at [[Fair Value]]. → [[Session 7 - Business Combination|Session 7]]

### Non-Controlling Interest
Minority shareholders' stake, shown separately in consolidated equity. → [[Session 7 - Business Combination|Session 7]]

### Consolidation
Combining 100% of a subsidiary's statements with the parent's; NCI splits income/equity. → [[Session 7 - Business Combination|Session 7]]

### Fair Value
The measurement basis for all assets/liabilities at the acquisition date. → [[Session 7 - Business Combination|Session 7]]

## Revenue recognition

### Revenue Recognition
The accounting process deciding when and how much revenue is recorded. → [[Session 8 - Revenue Recognition|Session 8]]

### ASC 606
The US GAAP converged revenue standard (5-step model). → [[Session 8 - Revenue Recognition|Session 8]]

### IFRS 15
The international converged revenue standard (5-step model). → [[Session 8 - Revenue Recognition|Session 8]]

### Performance Obligation
A promise to transfer a distinct good or service. → [[Session 8 - Revenue Recognition|Session 8]]

### Transaction Price
The consideration the entity expects to be entitled to. → [[Session 8 - Revenue Recognition|Session 8]]

### Variable Consideration
Discounts/bonuses/concessions, estimated and constrained. → [[Session 8 - Revenue Recognition|Session 8]]

### Standalone Selling Price
SSP — the basis for allocating the transaction price across obligations. → [[Session 8 - Revenue Recognition|Session 8]]

### Transfer of Control
The recognition trigger — point-in-time vs over-time. → [[Session 8 - Revenue Recognition|Session 8]]

### Deferred Revenue
A liability — cash received before delivery. → [[Session 8 - Revenue Recognition|Session 8]]

### Accounts Receivable
An unconditional right to consideration (billed, awaiting only the passage of time) — distinct from a contract asset and from [[Deferred Revenue]]. → [[Session 8 - Revenue Recognition|Session 8]]

### WACC
Weighted average cost of capital — the DCF discount rate. → [[Session 9 - Recap|Session 9]]

The discounted sum of future cash flows — the DCF output. → [[Session 9 - Recap|Session 9]]
