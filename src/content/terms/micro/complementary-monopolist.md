A **complementary monopolist** is a firm with monopoly power over *one component* of a [[Perfect Complements|complementary]] bundle — for instance, one of two sequential toll-road operators, or a video-game console maker selling alongside an independent game publisher. Each complementary monopolist sets the price of its component while taking the others' prices as given, leading to [[Double Marginalization]].

![](/images/micro/t4-complement-br.png)

Best responses in *prices* (not quantities). Both slope down, and the equilibrium $p_1 = p_2 = A/3$ gives a total $P = 2A/3$ — *higher* than the integrated monopoly price $A/2$.

## When to use

The complementary-monopolist label is what makes the Topic 4 result memorable: $N$ complementary monopolists yield $P^N = AN/(N+1)$, individual profit $A^2/(N+1)^2$, and total industry profit $NA^2/(N+1)^2$ — *maximised at $N=1$* and falling monotonically as the chain fragments. Use the term when contrasting with a *substitute*-goods oligopoly, where more firms drive price toward marginal cost.
