A **best response** is the optimal strategy for a player *given* a specific strategy chosen by the opponent. At a [[Nash Equilibrium]], every player is simultaneously playing a best response to everyone else's strategy.

## When to use

Underlining best responses is the practical method for finding pure-strategy Nash Equilibria in a matrix: for each opponent column, mark Player 1's payoff-maximising row; for each row, mark Player 2's payoff-maximising column; any doubly underlined cell is a NE (see the [[How to Find Nash Equilibria — Best Response Method]] recipe). Best responses generalise to *continuous* strategies as a [[Best Response Function]] — the curve whose intersection with the opponent's gives the equilibrium in Cournot, Bertrand, and complementary-pricing games.
