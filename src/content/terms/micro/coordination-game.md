A coordination game is one in which both players strictly prefer to coordinate on the *same* outcome rather than miscoordinate, but may disagree about *which* coordination outcome they prefer. The lecture covers two flavours: **Battle of the Sexes** (two pure NE, each player prefers a different one) and the **simple coordination game** where one equilibrium Pareto-dominates the other but the other is "safer" (risk-dominant).

## When to use

Recognise a coordination game whenever the matrix has multiple pure-strategy [[Nash Equilibrium|Nash Equilibria]] on the diagonal and the off-diagonal cells are strictly worse for both players. The exam-relevant subtlety: when one diagonal cell carries a catastrophic downside for off-equilibrium play (e.g. payoff $-100$), the *Pareto-dominant* equilibrium may not be the *risk-dominant* one — comment on both. A mixed-strategy NE also exists; solve by making each player make the *other* indifferent.
