Game theory is the formal study of strategic interaction — situations where each player's payoff depends not only on their own action but also on the actions chosen by the other players. A *game* specifies the players, the strategies available to each, and the payoffs for every possible combination of strategies.

## When to use

Use the game-theory framework whenever rational agents must decide while accounting for what other rational agents will decide. It is the language for oligopoly ([[Cournot Competition]], [[Bertrand Competition]], [[Stackelberg Model]]), bargaining, auction design, and any model where outcomes are interdependent.
