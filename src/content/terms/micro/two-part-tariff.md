A **two-part tariff** is a pricing scheme with a fixed entry fee $T$ plus a per-unit price $p$. The total a consumer pays for $Q$ units is $T + pQ$. With full information about a single consumer's demand, the *optimal* two-part tariff sets $p = MC$ (so consumption is efficient) and $T = CS$ at that price (so the firm captures *all* consumer surplus) — this is the constructive route to [[First-Degree Price Discrimination|perfect price discrimination]]. Classic examples: gyms (membership + per-class), amusement parks (entry + ride), Gillette razors (handle + blades).

## When to use

Use a two-part tariff whenever a question asks how to *capture all consumer surplus* from a single known consumer. With multiple consumer types and types unobservable, the two-part tariff becomes the building block for a [[Second-Degree Price Discrimination|self-selecting menu]] under [[Incentive Compatibility]] constraints — and the high-type consumer earns [[Information Rent]] equal to their consumer surplus on the low-type bundle.
