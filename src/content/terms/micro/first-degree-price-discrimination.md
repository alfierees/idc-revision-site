**First-degree (perfect) price discrimination** extracts the *entire* [[Consumer Surplus]] from each consumer. The constructive recipe is a [[Two-Part Tariff]] with $p = MC$ (so consumption is socially efficient) and a fixed entry fee $T = CS_i$ equal to the consumer's surplus at $p = MC$.

## When to use

Reach for first-degree PD as the upper benchmark on the seller's profit: any other pricing scheme leaves consumer surplus on the table. The real-world approximations are personalised pricing (think gym membership tailored to one consumer), bargained transactions, and full disclosure of willingness-to-pay. With *multiple* consumer types and types unobservable, first-degree PD is infeasible — the firm must fall back on [[Second-Degree Price Discrimination]] subject to [[Incentive Compatibility]] constraints.
